Ulm

Ulm is founded in medieval times. Visit the Ulmer Muenster, the Fishermen’s Quarter and stroll by the Danube river. The Ulmer Muenster is currently the tallest church in the world.

Tuebingen

Swabian university town combines the flair of a lovingly restored medieval centre with the colorful bustle and typical atmosphere of a young and cosmopolitan students' town. With two castles just outside the city, Tuebingen is definitely a place worth visit.


Burg Hohenzollern
Schloss Lichtenstein

Stuttgart

Stuttgart, capital of Baden-Wuerttemberg state, is known as a manufacturing hub. Mercedes-Benz and Porsche have headquarters and museums here. The city center is the perfect for shopping day or a night out.


Mercedes Benz Museum
Porsche Museum

Schloss Sigmaringen

Standing high on a cliff above the Danube, Sigmaringen Castle was built as a fortress. Over the centuries, it has been transformed into a royal residential palace, complete with fairytale towers and turrets, bay windows and terraces.

Schloss Neuschwanstein

The ULTIMATE Disney Castle!
Neuschwanstein Castle is a 19th-century historicist palace on a rugged hill above the village of Hohenschwangau near Fuessen in southwest Bavaria, Germany. The palace was commissioned by King Ludwig II of Bavaria as a retreat and in honour of Richard Wagner.
